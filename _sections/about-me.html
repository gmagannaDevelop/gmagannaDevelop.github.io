Born and raised in Guadalajara, Jalisco, México. Currently based in León, Guanajuato. I am a Biomedical Engineer, most representatively characterised by my curiosity. I love learning new things, be it languages, mathematics, programming or about anything. I picked my major because I wanted to partake in medical research but my favourite subject was always math. More than merely help to cure patients, I want my work to help improve the quality of life of chronically ill people like me (I have type 1 diabetes mellitus).

My main interests going into college were math and biology. I had no idea that Computer Science would become such a central topic of interest for me. At the Science and Engineering Division of the University of Guanajuato I had the privilege of working with talented and discilplined scientists who reshaped my vision of a professional career. I was also blessed to encounter friends with an unquenchable intellectual appetite. Together we learnt to program, beyond our programmes' scopes.

I studied abroad in Belgium for a whole academic year. My main a priori motivation was predominantly linguistic. Nevertheless, shortly after arriving to the city of Mons I found out that the year I had ahead of me was going to trascend my expectations in ways I could have not imagined. During that year I could mature and enlarge my software developing skills, creating applications that would help me change the way I manage my diabetes' therapy. Certainly what I least expected was to find the love of my life during the second semester of my exchange but these are the kind of things that we cannot control.

I am currently working on a project to further consolidate my expertise in time series processing techniques. The ultimate goal is to produce a set of high-quality literate and polyglot programming documents. These will serve as an educational asset freely available to the public and will set the foundation for futher research on glycaemic behaviour analysis. The organisation's repository: BioLogs
